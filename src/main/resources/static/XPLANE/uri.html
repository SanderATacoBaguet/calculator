Ohm's Law

How: V = I * R. Enter any two of the three variables to calculate the third.

Why: This formula relates voltage, current, and resistance in a circuit.

Where: Used to estimate currents, select resistor values, and design circuits.

Physical aspect: Shows how electron flow interacts with material resistance to create voltage drops.

Series Circuits

How: Total resistance = sum of all resistances.

Why: Current flows sequentially; resistances add linearly.

Where: Used in simple resistor networks or LED strings.

Physical aspect: Voltage drops across each resistor proportional to its resistance.

Parallel Circuits

How: 1/R_total = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ...

Why: Current divides inversely proportional to each branch resistance.

Where: Household wiring, parallel LEDs.

Physical aspect: Voltage across all branches is equal; total current is sum of branch currents.
